### IAM Role Structure

The IAM role will have the following structure:

1. **Trust Policy:** Allows only the Bedrock service to assume this role

- **Bedrock Permissions:**

- **Lambda Permissions:**

- The role is scoped to Bedrock agent resources only
- Permissions are limited to necessary actions (invoke models, invoke agents, invoke Lambda)

   - IAM Role resource
   - IAM Policy resource (attached to the role)

### Best Practices Applied

- **No Hardcoding:** Using AWS managed policy ARNs and service principals
- **Principle of Least Privilege:** Only granting necessary permissions
- **Service-Specific Access:** Restricting to Bedrock service only
- **Resource Scoping:** Limiting to Bedrock agent resources and Lambda functions

This architecture ensures that the IAM role is secure, properly scoped, and meets the business requirements for Bedrock service integration with Lambda functions.
