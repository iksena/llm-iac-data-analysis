## Architecture Plan

### IAM Role Design

The IAM role will be designed with the following characteristics:

1. **Trust Policy**: EC2 service as the trusted entity
2. **Permissions Policy**: Custom policy with read-only IAM permissions
3. **Policy Structure**: Explicit deny-by-default with specific read permissions

### IAM Policy Permissions

The custom IAM policy will include:

- `iam:List*` actions (ListUsers, ListRoles, ListPolicies, etc.)
- `iam:Get*` actions (GetUser, GetRole, GetPolicy, etc.)
- `iam:Generate*` actions (GenerateCredentialReport, GenerateOrganizationsAccessReport)
- `iam:SimulateCustomPolicy` for policy simulation
- `iam:SimulatePrincipalPolicy` for policy simulation

- Principle of least privilege: Only grant read-only access
- No write, modify, or delete permissions
- No IAM PassRole permissions to prevent privilege escalation

1. **Parameters** (if needed for future extensibility)

   - IAM Policy

- No hardcoded values
- No deprecated properties
- Proper use of dynamic references
- Correct IAM policy ARNs
- Proper resource protection policies
